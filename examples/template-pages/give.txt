<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:yellowtree/row {"template":"1","style":{"spacing":{"padding":{"top":"var:preset|spacing|2","bottom":"var:preset|spacing|6"}}}} -->
<!-- wp:yellowtree/column -->

<!-- wp:yellowtree/accordion-item {"showDescription":false} -->
<details><summary><h3 class="title">How ICF is financed?</h3></summary><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The ICF is financed according to biblical principles that have proven themselves since the Old Testament. God established tithing as the basis for providing for the temple—today, that is the church, where people encounter God, grow spiritually, and make a difference together. </p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></details>
<!-- /wp:yellowtree/accordion-item -->

<!-- wp:yellowtree/accordion-item -->
<details><summary><h3 class="title">What does tithing mean?</h3></summary><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The tithe is not a law, but a biblical principle: we give 10% of our income to the house of God to honor His provision. Today, it finances the salaries of pastors and staff, building costs, and the various areas of the church. </p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></details>
<!-- /wp:yellowtree/accordion-item -->

<!-- wp:yellowtree/accordion-item -->
<details><summary><h3 class="title">Does ICF itself also give a tithe?</h3></summary><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Yes! We at ICF also give 10% of our income to social projects, partner organizations, and the ICF Movement. We believe that what we sow will bring fruit!  </p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></details>
<!-- /wp:yellowtree/accordion-item -->

<!-- wp:yellowtree/accordion-item -->
<details><summary><h3 class="title">What is the first fruit?</h3></summary><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The first fruits are a powerful biblical principle of trust and devotion. We bring the first and best—not what is left over—as an expression of our faith that God will bless and multiply the rest. </p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></details>
<!-- /wp:yellowtree/accordion-item -->

<!-- wp:yellowtree/accordion-item -->
<details><summary><h3 class="title">What are voluntary offerings?</h3></summary><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>In the Old and New Testaments, God's people repeatedly made voluntary offerings to build churches and advance the kingdom of God. Through our annual “REACH BEYOND” campaign, we also make strategic investments in churches, social projects, and partners that go beyond our regular budget. </p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></details>
<!-- /wp:yellowtree/accordion-item -->

<!-- wp:yellowtree/accordion-item -->
<details><summary><h3 class="title">Are there any financial obligations or membership fees in the ICF?</h3></summary><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>No. The ICF does not have formal membership with financial obligations. Everyone is welcome, regardless of their financial situation.  </p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></details>
<!-- /wp:yellowtree/accordion-item -->

<!-- wp:yellowtree/accordion-item -->
<details><summary><h3 class="title">How does ICF handle donations?</h3></summary><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>ICF is committed to responsible and entrepreneurial financial management. Every donation is used in a targeted and purpose-specific manner to support the mission of the church in the long term. We act with foresight, invest wisely, and ensure that funds are used efficiently.  </p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></details>
<!-- /wp:yellowtree/accordion-item -->

<!-- wp:yellowtree/accordion-item -->
<details><summary><h3 class="title">Can one inspect the finances?</h3></summary><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Yes. Transparency is important to us—our finances can be viewed upon request, because trust is built through openness and integrity. </p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></details>
